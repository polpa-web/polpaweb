§ Part I — Understanding Polpiness
Polpino, -a /pol'pino/ noun
  1. The most adorable form of a cephalopod.
  2. A term of endearment referring to a squiddy being who enjoys both knuffels and coccole.
  3. A squishy blue puppet called 'Polpina' that was gifted to an Italian lady by a Dutch guy in Valencia.
Etymology: it. polpetta › polpina — first used in Valencia, when a Dutch man attempted the word 'polpetta' and made the cutest mistake.
Short forms: polpi, polpa, polpo
POLP /polp/ exclamation noun
  1. A universal form for any emotional reaction.
  2. Synonym of polpino, -a, often preceded by article.
"POLP!"
Contrary: TOK
Polpare /pol'pare/ verb [I]
  1. Activity that concerns the expression of affection.
  2. To exist in a form of full love and enjoy the presence of a fellow squid.
  3. Caddeling.
"Basta studiare, è giunta l'ora di polpare!"
Polpfriend /polp'frend/ noun
  1. A polp who is also a friend.
  2. More than a boyfriend or girlfriend: a polpfriend is a partner who gives eight times as many hugs and kisses.
  3. A life companion who shares your same passions (e.g. beers, Mirko Mariotti, AI Lodo song, pizza, zotty stuff, the goose game…)
"Having a Polpfriend is a squiddy experience, if you are willing to accept everlasting farts."
Polptrex /'polptrεks/ noun
  1. A polp who is also a t-rex.
  2. Refers to Miss Polpa Toso.
  3. The actual meaning of this expression is unknown; only Mr. Misja Polpelius has the answer.
Polpiful /'polpɪfʊl/ adjective
  1. Full of squiddy energy.
  2. A situation, person, activity, or entity that radiates the POLP factor.
Superlative: polpifulissimo, -a
"What do I think about the song Our House? Oh, it is absolutely polpiful!"
Polpiness /'polpines/ noun
  1. State of being a polp.
  2. Joyful feeling shared in various occasions by two squiddies.
  3. Quality possessed by anything that is jolly and squiddy.
Synonym: polpifulness
§ Part II — Polp Gastronomy
Polpcake /'polpkeɪk/ food
  1. Gooey recipe from the Netherlands, cooked with a squiddy twist.
  2. Salty or sweet, it always makes the belly happy with its crunch and softness.
  3. The best dinner!
"Can we make Polpcakes tonight?" / "But we had them yesterday, and the day before, and the day before, and the day before… ñam, of course we can!"
Polpfertjes /'polpfərtʃəs/ food
  1. Delicious and fluffy Nijntje-shaped mini polpcakes.
  2. The yummiest solution to fix a tokkie day.
  3. Little clouds of jolliness, often consumed with butter and powdered sugar.
"Ik houd van Polpfertjes! Kunnen we ze maken met onze Nijntje-Polpfertjespan van de Hema, de beste winkel ter world?"
Buon Polpetito /bwɔn polpe'tito/ expression
  1. Said before enjoying a pizza, pita falafel, polpcake, noodles, or anything gooey.
  2. From the Italian buon appetito, adapted for squiddy communication.
  3. Must be used only for meals shared with a fellow polpfriend.
Short form: buon polpe!
§ Part III — Polp Calendar
Polpiversary /polpivərsəri/ holiday
  1. The celebration of the day that the polps decided to swim together.
  2. 13th of September.
  3. An extremely polpiful day which is all about acting squiddy and jolly, giving each other presents and having dolci coccole.
"It's only the 14th of February, but I can't wait to say Happy Polpiversary!"
